Matt Bowen

MSc Physics & Space Science Candidate, RMC
BScH Physics & Computing, Queen's University

Hi there! I'm a highly motivated graduate of Queen's University with a Honours Bachelor's Degree in Physics and Computing. A technical mind and charismatic demeanor combine with excellent problem solving and communication skills. I derive knowledge and experience from three major avenues; academic, hobbyist, and professional: strong knowledge of computational methods applied to physics and software development, experienced in amateur electrical engineering in photovoltaics and space systems, and a professional team leader and decision-maker.

My History

Growing up a military kid, I grew fond of aviation and spaceflight, with the ultimate goal of becoming an astronaut. I bounced around various military towns, including a jaunt in Europe, before finishing my high school education at Mark Isfeld Secondary School in Courtenay, BC. Eventually, I grew too tall to be an astronaut and had to settle for the study of the cosmos while earthbound. I chose to attend Queen's University initially with a specialization in Astrophysics, however, after I took an intro to computing course, I discovered a second passion. I've always enjoyed creating things with my hands, and now I can do so with code as well!

Throughout my time at Queen's, I worked various odd jobs in other areas of interest to me. In my final year at Queen's, I worked at the Alma Mater Society as the Director of Information Technology, where I gained valuable experience in a practical setting. Upon the completion of my studies at Queen's, I decided to continue my education at the Royal Military College of Canada in Physics and Space Science, where I'll study remote sensing and space surveillance, and have the opportunity to work on cutting edge space projects.
